# Clean Communities and Anti-Litter Enforcement Act (HB 2526)

Rep. Day introduces legislation addressing vehicle littering, which he characterizes as
degrading highways and creating significant cleanup expenses. The proposed system would use
automated litter enforcement systems — complete with strong privacy protections, warning
signs, and due-process safeguards.

Key features:
- Authorizes PennDOT, the Turnpike Commission, and municipalities to operate such systems in
  designated zones.
- Establishes a $200 civil penalty with no driving-record points and no criminal consequences.
- Images deleted after citation issuance; fine revenues support cleanup programs.
- Requires clear warning signs before and after each zone, system calibration, operator
  training, and annual inspections.

Day frames the measure as strengthening community pride, protecting the environment, and
relieving the financial burden on taxpayers while respecting motorists' rights. He requests
co-sponsorship to demonstrate Pennsylvania's commitment to smart, targeted technology to keep
communities clean.

**Position signal:** Pro-clean-communities; comfortable with technology-enabled enforcement
*paired with* explicit privacy/due-process guardrails. Frames new enforcement as taxpayer relief.
