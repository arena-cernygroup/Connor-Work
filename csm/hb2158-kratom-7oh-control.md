# The Synthetic Kratom & 7-OH Control Act (HB 2158)

Companion legislation to Senate Bill 899 targeting synthetic kratom and 7-OH as Schedule I
controlled substances under Pennsylvania's Controlled Substance, Drug, Device and Cosmetic Act.

- Kratom-derived products are currently unregulated and widely available in retail and online.
- Natural kratom leaf remains unscheduled federally, but 7-OH — a concentrated synthetic
  derivative — produces powerful opioid-like effects. The FDA identified it as having
  significant abuse potential and recommended federal scheduling in July 2025.
- Child-safety concern: brightly packaged products resembling candy.
- PA poison control centers logged a sharp increase in calls after a Dept. of Health advisory
  (Aug. 4, 2025): patients aged 12 months to 80 years, naloxone administered in 25 cases, 14
  patients requiring mechanical ventilation.
- The bill targets only synthetic compounds while preserving naturally derived kratom leaf.

**Position signal:** Targeted regulation that closes a public-health gap without banning the
natural product — narrow, harm-specific intervention rather than a blanket prohibition.
