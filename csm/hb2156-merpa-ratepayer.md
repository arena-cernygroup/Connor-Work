# Municipal Electric Ratepayer Protection (MERPA) Act (HB 2156)

Legislation enabling any borough, township, city, or county to act as brokers on behalf of
their residents for electricity aggregation.

Key provisions:
- Municipalities may aggregate residential load on an opt-in basis without requiring an
  Electric Generation Supplier (EGS) license.
- Potential savings of 5–40% on electric generation for opt-in households.
- Municipal administrative fees capped at 1% of total savings.
- Participation entirely voluntary, no cost to residents.
- Full compliance with PUC requirements and consumer protections; annual PUC reporting and
  transparent billing.

Day frames it as strengthening municipal tools, protecting ratepayers, and delivering real
energy savings to PA families without mandating participation or risking public funds.

**Position signal:** Core "affordable energy + local control" issue. Voluntary, opt-in,
market-based mechanism with consumer protections — no mandates, no public-fund risk.
