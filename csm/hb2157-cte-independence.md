# CTE Independence Act (HB 2157)

Pennsylvania's 80+ Career and Technical Education (CTE) centers deliver industry-certified
training leading to high-wage, high-demand jobs, yet most remain under sending school district
control. Some districts restrict enrollment despite available capacity and waiting lists. This
structure hampers decision-making, limits employer partnerships, and slows adaptation to
workforce needs.

The bill transfers governance of each CTE school/institute to an independent governance board
within three years, preserving all state and local funding while providing autonomy.

Key provisions:
- Independent governance boards (minimum 5 members): industry reps, CTE staff, and parents.
- Full transfer of administrative, operational, and management authority from sending districts.
- Maintenance of existing state subsidies and local contributions.
- Full transparency: audits, public budgets, and open meetings.

Framed as granting the same local-control flexibility charter schools and community colleges
already enjoy — faster curriculum updates, stronger employer partnerships — "at zero cost to
taxpayers."

**Position signal:** Local-control + workforce-readiness; restructures governance without new
spending. Pro-autonomy, pro-employer-partnership, transparency-forward.
