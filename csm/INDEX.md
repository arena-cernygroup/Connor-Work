## What this corpus tells the Briefing Engine

Cross-cutting tendencies visible across his own bills — useful for the CONSISTENCY CHECK and
DISTRICT LENS sections:

- **Market-style mechanisms over mandates** — bonding (HB 2159), opt-in aggregation (HB 2156),
  independent boards (HB 2157). Prefers guarantees/incentives to bans.
- **Targeted, narrow scope** — kratom bill hits only synthetic 7-OH (HB 2158); litter
  enforcement is zone-limited with privacy guardrails (HB 2526).
- **Local control / municipal empowerment** is a through-line (HB 2156, HB 2157, HB 2159).
- **Taxpayer-cost framing** — repeatedly stresses "no cost to taxpayers" / taxpayer relief.
- **Warehouse oversight + affordable energy** are his signature responsible-growth issues,
  matching the 187th's warehouse/data-center pressures.
