# Decommissioning and Bonding of New Warehouses (HB 2159)

Rep. Day notes Pennsylvania's historical industrial prominence, then describes how abandoned
warehouses that once fueled the economy now dot the Commonwealth after economic shifts.

The bill would require owners of newly built warehouses to post financial guarantees for their
activities — a practice already established in other industries. Day emphasizes that warehouses
have a finite life span and at end-of-life may pose an environmental or public-health hazard if
not handled properly. He seeks to establish reasonable decommissioning and bonding amounts to
address warehouse dismantling.

**Position signal:** Centerpiece of "responsible growth / warehouse oversight" priority. Makes
developers internalize end-of-life cleanup costs via bonding — market-style guarantee rather
than a building ban. Directly relevant to the 187th's warehouse/data-center pressures.
